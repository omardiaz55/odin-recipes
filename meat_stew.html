Meat Stew

Description

Dominican sancocho is a traditional and hearty stew that holds a special place in Dominican cuisine. It's often considered the national dish of the Dominican Republic and is cherished for its rich flavors and cultural significance. Sancocho is typically enjoyed during gatherings, celebrations, and family events. There are various regional variations of sancocho across the Dominican Republic

Ingredients

  • Assorted meats (such as chicken, beef, pork, and/or goat), cut into pieces
  • Plantains, peeled and sliced
  • Yuca (cassava), peeled and cut into chunks
  • Sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • Corn on the cob, cut into smaller pieces
  • Green plantains, peeled and sliced
  • Celery, chopped
  • Carrots, peeled and sliced
  • Bell peppers, chopped
  • Onions, chopped
  • Garlic, minced
  • Cilantro, chopped
  • Oregano, dried or fresh
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chicken or beef broth
  • Water
  • Olive oil or cooking fat

Steps

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil or cooking fat over medium heat. Add chopped onions, bell peppers, and garlic. Saut√© until the onions become translucent and fragrant.
  2. Add the assorted meats to the pot and brown them on all sides.
  3. Pour in enough chicken or beef broth to cover the meats, and add water as needed to create a flavorful base for the stew.
  4. Season with oregano, salt, and pepper. Let the meats simmer until they start to become tender.
  5. Add the yuca (cassava), plantains, sweet potatoes, and carrots to the pot. These ingredients contribute to the heartiness of the sancocho.
  6. Continue simmering the stew until the yuca and other root vegetables start to soften.
  7. Stir in the sliced green plantains and corn on the cob pieces. These ingredients add additional textures and flavors to the dish.
  8. Allow the sancocho to simmer gently until all the ingredients are fully cooked and tender. The stew should have a thick and rich consistency.
  9. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed, adding more salt, pepper, or herbs as desired.
  10. Just before serving, stir in chopped cilantro to enhance the freshness of the flavors.

Serving

Serve the Dominican sancocho hot in large bowls. It's often accompanied by white rice, avocado slices, and a side of sliced limes or sour oranges. The combination of meats, root vegetables, and aromatic seasonings creates a deeply satisfying and comforting dish that's both delicious and culturally significant. Please note that this description is a general overview of Dominican sancocho, and there may be variations in ingredients and preparation methods based on individual preferences and regional influences.
